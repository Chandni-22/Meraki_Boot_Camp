Tribute to Sarojini Naidu

Introduction

Sarojini Naidu (1879â€“1949) was an Indian independence activist, poet, and politician. She was the first woman to become the President of the Indian National Congress and the first woman to become the Governor of an Indian state.

Early Life

Sarojini Naidu was born on February 13, 1879, in Hyderabad, India. She was educated in Chennai, London, and Cambridge. Her father, Aghorenath Chattopadhyay, was a scientist and philosopher, and her mother, Barada Sundari Devi, was a poet and a Bengali Brahmo.

Political Career

Naidu became involved in the Indian independence movement in the early 1900s. She was a close associate of Mahatma Gandhi and was known for her eloquent speeches and writings. She played a leading role in the Salt Satyagraha and the Quit India Movement.

Literary Contributions

Alongside her political activities, Sarojini Naidu was also a renowned poet. Her poetry was known for its lyrical beauty and passionate patriotism. Some of her notable works include "The Golden Threshold," "The Bird of Time," and "The Broken Wing."

"We want deeper sincerity of motive, a greater courage in speech and earnestness in action."

Legacy

Sarojini Naidu's contributions to Indian society and culture are widely recognized. She was a trailblazer for women's rights and played a significant role in shaping the future of India. Her legacy continues to inspire generations of Indians.
