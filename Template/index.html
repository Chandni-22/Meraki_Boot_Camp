MY FAVOURITE CARTOON SHOWS

Courage the Cowardly Dog

"Courage the Cowardly Dog" is an animated television
series that aired on Cartoon Network from 1999 to
2002. Created by John R. Dilworth, the show follows
the adventures of Courage, a small, pink, and easily
frightened dog who lives in a farmhouse with his
elderly owners, Muriel and Eustace Bagge, in the 
fictional town of Nowhere. Despite his fears, Courage
often finds himself confronting supernatural creatures,
monsters, aliens, and other bizarre phenomena in
order to protect his owners from harm. Courage's 
catchphrase, "The things I do for love," 
became iconic among fans of the series. "Courage the
Cowardly Dog" has gained a cult following and is
regarded as one of Cartoon Network's classic
    animated shows.

Johnny Test

"Johnny Test" follows the adventures of a boy named Johnny Test who serves as a test subject for his genius twin sisters, Susan and Mary, who are both brilliant scientists. Johnny often finds himself involved in their experiments, which frequently lead to chaotic and humorous situations. Johnny's loyal and talking dog, Dukey, also plays a significant role in the show, providing comedic relief and occasionally helping Johnny out of sticky situations. "Johnny Test" gained popularity during its original run and has been praised for its energetic animation style and entertaining storylines. It has a dedicated fanbase, among children and teenagers.
